[Narrator]: The old clock tower chimed nine as Alice stepped into the dimly lit archive room.

[Alice]: Hello? Is anyone here? I got a message saying someone wanted to see me urgently.

[Bob]: I'm glad you came. Sit down, please. What I'm about to tell you can't leave this room.

[Alice]: You're scaring me. What's going on?

[Bob]: Three days ago, a set of documents went missing from this archive. Documents that could change everything we know about the founding of this city.

[Narrator]: Alice felt the color drain from her face. She had been the last one in this room three days ago.

[Alice]: What kind of documents are we talking about?

[Bob]: Letters. Private correspondence between the mayor and a man who, according to every official record, died in 1943. The handwriting on both sides is identical.

[Alice]: That's not possible.

[Bob]: No. It shouldn't be. And yet here we are.

[Narrator]: Silence settled between them like dust on forgotten shelves.

[Alice]: Why did you call me specifically?

[Bob]: Because your name was in those letters, Alice. Written seventy years before you were born.
